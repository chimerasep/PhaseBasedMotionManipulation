Phase-Based Motion Manipulation

Reducing Cybersickness in Virtual Reality

About the Project

Our project addresses the critical issue of cybersickness in virtual reality environments by manipulating motion to reduce perceived intensity. By adapting phase-based motion techniques, we aim to create more comfortable and accessible VR experiences for users.

How It Works

Phase Analysis

Our system analyzes motion sequences in VR environments by breaking them down into distinct phases, allowing for precise manipulation of movement patterns to reduce perceived intensity.

Real-Time Processing

We process frames from Unity in real-time, ensuring minimal latency while maintaining the quality of motion manipulation for a seamless VR experience.

Implementation

Our solution builds on existing phase-based motion techniques, adapted and optimized for VR environments to reduce cybersickness while preserving immersion.

Technical Approach

Based on research by Wadhwa et al. (2013) and the Riesz pyramid representation, our system captures frames from Unity, processes them using phase-based techniques, and returns modified frames with reduced motion intensity. This approach helps mitigate the sensory conflict that causes cybersickness while maintaining the immersive nature of VR experiences.
